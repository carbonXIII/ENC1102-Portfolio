Intertextuality

Overview

Students will be able to analyze and synthesize complex texts in ways that demonstrate an understanding of the situated and intertextual nature of writing and research.

An important threshold concept, built upon throughout the class, is the concept of intertextuality; the idea that texts are part of an ongoing conversation within a discourse community.

Examples

In the first part of the research project, we identified a few major sources in our topic area, and used the ideas about intertextuality to find parallels between the texts. Looking through the "Literature Review" section of the research proposal, I identified common themes in papers about open source documentation. Another instance where intertextuality came into play, was referring to secondary sources in the discussions section of the article itself.

Research

Overview

Students will engage in a recursive, inquiry-based writing and research process that is meaningful for a specific community.

Once a rhetor becomes aware of an ongoing conversation within a topic area, the next step is to learn how to take part in this conversation themselves. Finding areas to add to the conversation involves building off of existing recurring themes in a discourse community and using them to build an original idea or to collect additional information to fuel the creation of new theories.

Examples

In the course, one key area in which this concept is applied is the first few paragraphs of the theoretical framework section of the reserach proposal. In this section, the approach to the study – the lens through which collected information will be applied to gain insights in the topic area – is defined.

Analysis

Overview

Students will be able to interpret their research findings in order to produce arguments that matter to specific communities by addressing real-world exigencies.

Once the data has been collected, and patterns have been identified, the process of drawing insights from those patterns brings us back to the concepts surrounding intertextuality. After directing effort in choosing research questions and collecting data towards the ongoing conversation up to this point, effort continues to go towards building conclusions that are similarly meaningful to our topic area.

Examples

In the conclusions section of the research paper, the conclusions drawn had real-world application: a maintainer or contributor to an open source repository could adjust their documentation to better reflect the model shared by more active and successful repositories.

… These mechanisms work together to create repositories with the features exhibited in the data; features supporting clearer documentation and maintainable contributions. By focusing on reproducing these mechanisms, other open source projects can gain success through developer contribution.

An example of practical application of findings from the Research Article.

Writing Concepts

Overview

Students will examine their own conceptions of writing and research in response to their inquiry, reading, and writing throughout the course.

Examples

In the beginning of the course, we analyzed Miller's writing in Genre as Social Action. An insight I gained from that, was the idea that the common need to express some idea, or take some action within a discourse community shaped the development of a genre. Later on, I built on this idea by suggesting that by looking at examples of successful texts, one could gain insights about what features of a genre lead to that success. This is described in the theoretical framework section of the research article.

Miller believes that the action – what the author wants to achieve through writing – is the main impetus behind the development of genres.

An excerpt from my response to 'Discussion Post: Genre'.

One of the initial assumptions going into this is that in an open source setting, direct communication with prospective users is not usually possible, so popular open source repositories must have effective documentation to convince developers to take an interest. This study takes advantage of this to find samples representative of effective documentation.
